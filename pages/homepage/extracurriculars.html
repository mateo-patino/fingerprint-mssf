These are my top 3 favorite extracurriculars!
  1. During my sophomore year, I was reading a book about cryptocurrencies and stumbled upon the Secure Hashing Algorithm 256-bit (SHA-256). I enjoyed reading about this algorithm so much that I ended up writing a 28-page research paper about it and publishing it in the educational Mexican journal Vision Educativa IUNAES. My paper did not have a scientific/discovery-oriented goal but a pedagogical one: I realized there was very little literature about SHA-256 that a cryptography enthusiast without the expertise of an undegraduate or graduate degree could understand, so I decided to write a paper explaining the algorithm in the simplest yet most complete way possible by prioritizing its beginner-oriented delivery and language. You can download the paper here.
  2. Bogota's air quality is lousy. To raise awareness about this issue at my school, I partnered with two of my friends to build a web application that retrieves hourly air quality data from the World Air Quality Index Project's website and stores it in a free-access database. We aimed to use this database to create a historical record of Bogota's and our school's air quality.
  3. Richard Feynman said that the best way to learn something is to teach it. I agree with him! A couple of years ago, I was incredibly bad at math, but I discovered that I was just being intellectually lazy and that I could become very proficient at math if I tried to teach it to others. Today, I run a small YouTube channel that I'm very proud of and that allows me to reinforce and organize my thoughts about many mathematical concepts in calculus, algebra, and more!
